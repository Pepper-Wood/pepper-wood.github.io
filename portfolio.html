Adoptapedia PHP, HTML, CSS, JS, SQL

Adoptapedia

Adoptapedia - short for Adoptable Encyclopedia - is an online database to help artists who create and sell character designs called "adoptables". I started this project in 2012 beginning with an excel spreadsheet by copying and pasting group names. Once I began learning to code, I wrote the application first with Java and have since rewritten in Python, while manually updating the readouts of these programs to the DeviantArt group linked above. The project's current incarnation is found at its live website, with continuous active development as we add new features. The ultimate goal of the project is to provide a central hub for members of the adoptable community by providing resources as well as ways to connect with other artists. More information about the project and its contributors can be found at the live website under the About tab.

Fall 2015, this was a group computer graphics project focused on learning to use various javascript graphics engines. Once initializing the Grid Size and setting cube states to Random, a rotating grid of cubes of random colors spins on the lower part of the screen. These cube states then activate and deactivate following the rules in John Conway's game of life. The button at the top of the screen adds a fun dimension by switching the random colors of the cubes to images of Professional Wrestler John Cena as his theme song plays over. In Fall 2016, the interface was redesigned by myself to be more user-friendly as well as challenge myself to utilize color palettes. All versions of the interface are shown in the slideshow above.

FreezeAlert Python

FreezeAlert

Spring 2015, this was a group engineering project to create a solution to pipes freezing and bursting in houses especially during the winter. Our physical units consisted of two raspberry pis - one to act as the central control and the other to be mounted on a pipe in a household with a temperature censor. My contributions was the entirety of the code for the project, which focussed on: enabling communication between these two units, create a tracking graph of the temperature of the pipe over time, and turn on an off a motor to help run water through the pipes and prevent it from being standstill. The above image is a complete look at our final project incarnation, featuring two systems encased in plastic with arduinos, an LED display, a temperature censor, and a simple rotary motor.
